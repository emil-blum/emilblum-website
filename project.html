CASE STUDY: 01

Deconstructing the Digital Magazine:
A Study in Reduction.

When Mono/Type approached us, they were suffocating under the weight of their own content. The challenge wasn't just to redesign a website; it was to rethink how we consume long-form editorial content in an age of infinite scroll.

The Architecture of Silence

We began by stripping away everything that wasn't content. No sidebars, no popups, no distraction. We established a strict grid system inspired by Swiss design principles of the 1960s, but adapted for fluid responsive viewports. The whitespace became an active element, not just empty space.

Typography played the central role. We selected a grotesque sans-serif for headlines to provide structural weight, paired with a highly legible serif for body copy to encourage sustained reading. The contrast creates a rhythm that guides the eye down the page without fatigue.

Interaction Design

The interface recedes as the user engages. Navigation elements fade out on scroll, returning only when the user signals intent by scrolling up. We implemented custom smooth-scrolling physics to give weight to the movement, making the digital experience feel tactile, almost paper-like.

Technical Execution

  • Headless CMS architecture for content portability.
  • WebGL transitions for image galleries to maintain performance.
  • Variable fonts to optimize load times and rendering precision.
